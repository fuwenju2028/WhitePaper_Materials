## Abstract

For three hundred years, we have divided real numbers into rationals and irrationals. For one hundred years, we have further divided irrationals into algebraic and transcendentals. Today, we stand at a new cognitive frontier.

The decimal expansion of π has been computed to tens of trillions of digits without any discovery of a repeating cycle. But beneath the surface of "non-repeating and infinite," might there exist another kind of order—not simple repetition, but complex, multi-scale, and continuously unfolding structure?

Through systematic analysis of 100 million digits of π, this study has uncovered evidence supporting this conjecture: π passes all classical randomness tests while exhibiting a Hurst exponent significantly deviating from 0.5 (H≈0.5049, p<10⁻²⁶), a power spectrum containing nearly sixty thousand significant periodic peaks, and a multi-scale fluctuation scaling exponent (H_h≈-0.009) qualitatively different from that of white noise (-0.5).

Based on these findings, we propose the concept of "Structural Irrational Numbers," further refined as **"Endogenously Structural Expanding Transcendentals"** —a nomenclature that characterizes π and its kin from four dimensions: origin (endogenous), form (structural), mode of manifestation (expanding), and mathematical category (transcendental).

π is no longer a solitary landmark on the wasteland of irrational numbers, but the first coordinate of a new mathematical continent. This manifesto is both a first map of that continent and a sincere invitation to mathematical explorers worldwide.

## 1. A Fundamental Question

In 2019, the Google Cloud team computed π to 31.4 trillion digits after the decimal point. No cycle. No end.

For three hundred years, we have been doing one thing: computing the next digit of π. We assumed that if we only computed enough, we would eventually "see through" it. But the silence of three hundred trillion digits tells us: perhaps we have been asking the wrong question.

**"Non-repeating and infinite" logically negates periodicity, but makes no assertion about internal structure.**

A more fundamental question is: If a sequence is infinite, non-repeating, and passes all classical randomness tests, must it necessarily be structureless? Or might it possess a higher-order order—one we have not yet learned to recognize?

This manifesto attempts to respond to this core question.

---

## 2. A Paradigm Shift: From Outward to Inward

Facing this cognitive wall, a fundamental paradigm shift is necessary.

### 2.1 From "Outward Conquest" to "Inward Exploration"

We stop asking "What is the Nth digit?" and instead ask: **"How do the digits relate to one another?"**

The object of study transforms from a passive list of numbers into a dynamic network of relationships. Depth is no longer defined by the number of digits, but by the complexity of structural relationships.

### 2.2 From "Digits" to "A Self-contained Text"

We view the expansion of π as a magnum opus continuously written by some deep "grammar." Our task, like deciphering an unknown manuscript, is to reverse-engineer its internal generative rules and narrative structure from finite fragments.

### 2.3 Core Insight: "Structural Randomness"

The sequence of π passes all classical randomness tests—uniform digit distribution (p=0.609), runs pattern meeting expectations (p=1.000), minimal autocorrelation structure. This "random veil" is so perfect that its very existence suggests the presence of a higher-order order hidden beneath.

We term this **"Structural Randomness"** —an intrinsic property that simulates randomness macroscopically to pass tests, while revealing systematic, non-trivial patterns under finer statistical scrutiny.

## 5. The Contours of Evidence: What We Have Seen

### 5.1 π Remembers Its Own Past

Hurst exponent H = 0.504946, statistically significantly deviating from the theoretical value of 0.5 for memoryless random sequences (p < 10⁻²⁶).

This means: the digit generation of π is not a "memoryless" independent process. The current Nth digit is weakly but systematically influenced by its distant history (up to millions of digits away).

### 5.2 The Spectrum of π Is Not White Noise

The power spectrum of π contains 58,746 significant periodic peaks, while a random sequence of the same length contains only about 102. Spectral flatness γ=0.372 (random sequence ≈0.998).

This means: beneath the "random veil" of π lies a "forest" of tens of thousands of periodic patterns.

### 5.3 The Fluctuations of π Do Not Decay with Scale

The multi-scale fluctuation scaling exponent H_h ≈ -0.009, while the theoretical value for white noise is -0.5.

This means: the fluctuation intensity of π is nearly constant across different time scales—information is "equally" distributed across all scales. This is a unique statistical structure that has not been fully studied.

### 5.4 The Synergy of Evidence

The above pieces of evidence are not isolated. They jointly point to a self-consistent portrait:

- **Processuality** (long-range memory) provides structure along the time dimension
- **Hierarchy** (scale-invariant fluctuations) provides structure along the scale dimension
- **Nesting** (containment of periods) provides structure along the spatial dimension
- **Controlled Randomness** (random veil + structural core) serves as the overarching meta-property

## 6. What This Means

### 6.1 Implications for Mathematics

- **Extension of taxonomy**: Adding a "structural" dimension to the rational/irrational dichotomy
- **Deepening of the normal number conjecture**: The strong inclusiveness of π supports its normality and suggests possible stratification of structural strength among normal numbers
- **New research paradigm**: From "searching for cycles" to "characterizing internal structure"

### 6.2 Implications for Applications

- **Cryptography**: The deterministic complex sequences of "endogenously structural expanding transcendentals" can serve as entropy sources for new types of pseudo-random number generators
- **Complex system modeling**: π's sequence as a benchmark model with long memory, multi-scale structure, and endogenous complexity
- **Artificial intelligence**: Inspiring new neural network initialization methods or architectures

### 6.3 Philosophical Implications

We stand at the boundary between two interpretations:

- **Mirror of cognition**: Are the "structures" we discovered projections of the human mind's inherent pattern-recognition modules onto infinite complexity?
- **Whisper of being**: Does the infinitely rich, self-consistent, dynamic structure of π suggest it is a discovered, **self-existent complex mathematical entity**?

Whichever answer one leans toward, the concept of "Endogenously Structural Expanding Transcendentals" compels us to rethink the ancient and fundamental questions of **order and randomness, finitude and infinity, mathematical discovery and physical reality.**
